Research Question

Can diffusion attention track?

DiffTrack asks whether a generative video diffusion model has already learned enough spatial correspondence to act as a tracker. Instead of training on labeled tracking data, it extracts query-key attention maps from CogVideoX and turns those maps into point trajectories.

Task

Given a query point in frame 0, predict its position across later frames in a video.

Method

Add diffusion noise, run CogVideoX, capture attention maps, and decode weighted coordinates.

Metric

Mean pixel error, reported as delta_avg and distance-specific errors delta_1 through delta_16.

Strengths

What works well

Zero tracking supervision

DiffTrack extracts correspondence from a generative model trained for video synthesis, avoiding labeled tracking data.

Strong short-range precision

The best configuration reaches 0.8 px error at a 1-frame gap, showing very sharp local correspondence.

Actionable internals

Layer and timestep ablations reveal where spatial precision and cross-frame attention are strongest.

Limitations

Targeted experiments

We ran controlled experiments on DAVIS videos using the released checkpoint. Each probe changes one variable so the failure mode is visible rather than speculative.

01

Timestep sensitivity

Setup: Compare t=1, t=10, t=30, and t=49 at layer 17.

Finding: t=10 achieves 13.1 delta_avg, a 72% improvement over the t=49 default at 47.0.

Failure: t=1 produces complete failure, showing the tracker depends on diffusion noise.

02

Layer brittleness

Setup: Compare attention extraction at layers 5, 8, 17, 27, and 29.

Finding: Layer 5 reaches 31.3 delta_avg, beating the paper's layer 17 default by 34%.

Failure: Performance varies by roughly 50%, so deployment requires dataset-specific layer search.

03

Temporal degradation

Setup: Measure errors at frame gaps delta_1, delta_2, delta_4, delta_8, and delta_16.

Finding: The best configuration grows from 0.8 px at a 1-frame gap to 73.8 px at a 16-frame gap.

Failure: The method lacks motion memory, so large displacement quickly breaks correspondence.

Evidence

Key results

Final interpretation

DiffTrack is genuinely impressive as evidence that video diffusion transformers encode correspondence. But the experiments show that this capability is fragile: it depends on the right noise level, the right layer, and short temporal gaps. The method is strongest as a representation probe and weakest as a deployable long-horizon tracker.
