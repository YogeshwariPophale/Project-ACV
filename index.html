Part A

Parameter Study: Strengths

Part A tested three key parameters to understand which configurations produce the best tracking, and to verify the paper's claimed design choices.

Experiment 1: Layer Selection

What we tested: Which of the 42 transformer layers produces the best attention maps for tracking? We tested layers 5, 17, and 27, all at timestep t=49.

Layer Position delta_avg delta_1 delta_8 delta_16 vs Baseline
Layer 5 Early (shallow) 31.3 0.8 51.7 73.8 -34% better
Layer 17 Mid (paper default) 47.0 7.0 74.6 81.2 Baseline
Layer 27 Deep 37.0 3.7 57.5 73.8 -21% better

Finding 1: Layer 5 beats the paper's recommended layer 17 by 34%. Shallow layers preserve fine spatial detail that deep layers abstract away.

Experiment 2: Timestep Selection

What we tested: At which diffusion timestep should attention maps be extracted? We tested t=1, t=10, t=30, and t=49.

Timestep Noise Level delta_avg delta_1 delta_8 delta_16 vs Baseline
t=1 Fully clean 0.0 0.0 0.0 0.0 Complete fail
t=10 Lightly noisy 13.1 0.7 22.1 25.1 -72% better
t=30 Moderately noisy 47.8 6.5 73.8 80.9 +2% worse
t=49 Heavily noisy (paper) 47.0 7.0 74.6 81.2 Baseline

Finding 2: t=1 fails completely, while t=10 gives a 72% improvement over t=49. There is a noise sweet spot: enough noise to activate correspondence, but not so much that features become imprecise.

Experiment 3: Chunk Frame Interval

What we tested: Whether an overlapping 13-frame sliding window meaningfully improves tracking on longer videos.

Configuration delta_avg Difference
With chunk frame interval 47.0 Baseline
Without chunk frame interval 46.4 -0.6 px, marginal

Chunking makes almost no difference. The limitation is architectural, not something a windowing heuristic fixes.

Part B

Limitations

Part B tested configurations designed to expose failure modes. The project probed three architectural weaknesses: noise dependency, layer brittleness, and catastrophic temporal degradation.

Limitation 1: Complete Failure Without Noise

What we tested: What happens at t=1, where the latent is fully denoised?

Root Cause 1: Noise Dependency

The cross-frame correspondence mechanism exists because of denoising. Too little noise fails; too much noise degrades feature precision.

Root Cause 2: Instantaneous Matching

Each frame is independently compared to frame 0. There is no memory, velocity estimate, or trajectory model.

Root Cause 3: No Layer Criterion

Point tracking needs fine spatial precision, but the method provides no reliable way to select the best layer for a new domain.

New Findings Not in the Original Paper

  • Timestep t=10 is a sweet spot and improves delta_avg by 72% over t=49.
  • Cross-attention saturates early, so running to t=49 wastes compute for tracking.
  • Layer 17 is the worst layer among the five tested configurations.
